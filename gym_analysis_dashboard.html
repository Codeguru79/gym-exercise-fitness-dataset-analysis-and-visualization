02 — LINEAR REGRESSION MODELS
Simple Linear Regression Analysis
Three regression models were built using Scikit-learn (80/20 train-test split, random_state=42) to study predictors of calorie burn and fat percentage.
Model 1 — Session Duration → Calories Burned
y = β₀ + β₁ · Session_Duration(hours)  |  80/20 split
✓ STRONG MODEL
R² SCORE
0.813
MSE
13,920
HYPOTHESIS
Reject H₀
Interpretation: 81.3% of the variance in calories burned is explained by session duration alone — a highly predictive model. As workout time increases, calories burned increases proportionally. The MSE of 13,920 is acceptable given that calorie values range from ~300 to ~1800. We reject H₀ and accept H₁: a significant relationship exists.
Model 2 — Avg BPM → Calories Burned
y = β₀ + β₁ · Avg_BPM  |  80/20 split
⚠ WEAK MODEL
R² SCORE
0.136
MSE
64,368
HYPOTHESIS
Weak Evidence
Interpretation: Only 13.6% of calorie variance is explained by average heart rate — a weak predictor. The high MSE of 64,368 confirms poor accuracy. Heart rate alone is insufficient; other factors like session duration, weight, and workout type have far greater influence. We fail to strongly reject H₀.
Model 3 — BMI → Fat Percentage
y = β₀ + β₁ · BMI  |  80/20 split
✗ NO RELATIONSHIP
R² SCORE
0.003
MSE
38.25
HYPOTHESIS
Accept H₀
Interpretation: R² ≈ 0 means BMI explains virtually none of the variance in fat percentage in this dataset. Despite low MSE (38.25), the model is not meaningful. This is a strong analytical finding — BMI, while a common health proxy, is not a reliable fat % predictor here. We accept H₀: no significant relationship.

04 — CONCLUSION & FUTURE SCOPE
Key Findings
The analysis reveals clear hierarchy among predictors of fitness outcomes — with session duration as the dominant factor.
⏱️

Session Duration is King

R² = 0.813 confirms workout duration explains 81% of calorie variance — the strongest single predictor found.

💓

Heart Rate ≠ Predictor

Avg BPM alone yields R² = 0.136, confirming that intensity metric alone is insufficient to predict calorie burn.

⚖️

BMI Fails Fat% Prediction

R² ≈ 0.003 shows BMI is not a reliable fat percentage predictor in real gym data — a meaningful real-world finding.

♀️♂️

Gender Matters for Calories

T-tests confirm males burn significantly more calories (avg 944 vs 866), even within the same session duration bracket.

🫀

Heart Rate: Gender-Neutral

No significant difference in average BPM between males and females — physiological heart rate response is similar.

🔗

Pearson r = 0.907

Session duration & calories have a very strong positive correlation — nearly a perfect linear relationship.
